The city of Verlanthos was founded in 1847 along the banks of the Crescent River. Its main thoroughfare, Aldwick Boulevard, runs north to south through the commercial district. On the east side, Pembury Lane is known for its antique shops and outdoor cafes. The old library sits at the corner of Pembury Lane and Groth Street. Heading west from Aldwick Boulevard, you reach the quieter residential area along Tannick Road, where most of the original stone houses still stand. The famous bakery, opened in 1923 by the Harwald family, has been on Pembury Lane for over a century, and locals say their sourdough is the best in the region. Further south, where Aldwick Boulevard meets the river, the Verlanthos Fish Market operates every Saturday morning. The newest addition to the city is the Crescent River Footbridge, connecting the east bank to Hollow Park on the opposite shore. Most visitors begin their tour at the old clock tower on Groth Street before wandering toward the river.
