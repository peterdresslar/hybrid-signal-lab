The Westbridge Community Theater announced its spring production, a musical called "The Starlight Revue" requiring five lead roles: the Captain, the Merchant, the Scholar, the Gardener, and the Fool. After three rounds of auditions, the director posted the cast list: Marcus would play the Captain, Sylvia would play the Merchant, James the Scholar, Priya the Gardener, and Tom the Fool. The ensemble was rounded out with twelve supporting actors and a live pit orchestra.

During the first week of rehearsal, Sylvia informed the director that she had a scheduling conflict with two of the three performance dates and would need to withdraw entirely. The director asked Priya to take over the Merchant role instead, since her vocal range was a better fit for the part's demanding second-act aria. Priya agreed, leaving the Gardener role uncast. James recommended his understudy, Nora, for the Gardener, and the director approved after a brief callback audition.

Two days before opening night, Marcus twisted his ankle during the dress rehearsal and could not perform the Captain's physically demanding scenes. Tom, who had been quietly memorizing all five parts throughout the rehearsal process, volunteered to step into the Captain role. The director agreed and quickly reorganized: Nora was moved from the Gardener to the Fool, since she had stronger comedic timing than anyone else available, and a newcomer named Leo was brought in to play the now-vacant Gardener.
