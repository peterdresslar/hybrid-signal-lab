The Riverside Chess Club held its annual knockout tournament with eight players seeded into a single-elimination bracket. In the first round, four matches were played simultaneously in the club's main hall. On board one, Garcia defeated Thompson in a sharp Sicilian Defense that lasted 34 moves, ending with a rook-and-knight mating pattern. On board two, Kim drew the first classical game against Nakamura but won the rapid tiebreak convincingly with black, advancing on differential. Board three featured the longest game of the round: Patel played a grueling endgame against Williams, converting a bishop-versus-knight advantage after 67 painstaking moves. Finally, on board four, newcomer Osei produced the upset of the tournament by beating top-seeded Johansson with a brilliant queen sacrifice on move 23 that left the spectators applauding.

In the semifinals, the bracket paired the first-round winners in sequence. Garcia faced Kim in a tense positional struggle dominated by maneuvering in a closed center. Garcia eventually found a crucial pawn break on the queenside that opened lines to Kim's king, and Kim resigned on move 41. The other semifinal saw Patel face Osei in what many spectators called the most entertaining game of the entire event. Osei, riding the confidence of his first-round upset, launched an aggressive kingside attack straight out of the opening, but Patel's solid preparation in the Caro-Kann Defense neutralized every threat. When the dust settled, Patel had emerged with an extra rook and converted the endgame cleanly to advance.
