# Companion Paper: Kantian Individuals, Superorganisms, and Artificial Intelligence

## Argument Outline

We propose the notion of Kantian individuals; a synthesis of three established frameworks for individuality offered by Krakauer et al. (2020), Kauffman and Roli (2025), and Hoel (2025). Kantian individuality provides a testable framework for identifying a specific class of entities, the known members of which are limited to Earth's organisms. Other evolved or constructed Kantian individuals may exist in the universe, and the framework provides a means of identifying them as similar in specific ways to life on Earth.

We propose that the often-redefined term "superorganism" can be more concretely applied as referring to a specific class of entities that fail the Kantian individuality test only through a lack of constraint closure. Examples that appear to fit this definition include eusocial insects such as bees or ant colonies: for which the superorganism definition fits specifically to the colonies themselves. We observe that eusocial insects are imperilled by removal from the colony, but do not immediately die. A thought experiment considering a condition or subspecies of a eusocial insect in which such exclusion did indeed result in immediate death should yield the result that such a condition would convert the colony into a single organism. A resulting conclusion is that individual member insects are indeed different from other organisms in important ways. Extending this thought should indicate that a superorganism does not need to be composed of living objects to meet the conditions of individuality sensu Krakauer et al. (2020) and causality sensu Hoel (2025).

Connections between the development of artificial intelligence and the creation of living organisms have been discussed for at least a century. But, as Kauffman and Roli (2025) demonstrate, while the development of organisms from autocatalytic sets is predictable, it is also necessarily very slow. Eusocial insects such as bees exemplify a different path for developing a causally-primary, information-producing individual. We examine how AI research and development might consider a shift in focus from developing synthetic organisms to developing synthetic superorganisms, and what ramifications such a shift might signal for research priorities and engineering.

## Notes

- It is assumed that all of the entities we test are collective on some level. In a private work, I define collective as: 
> a group of two or more information-processing entities embedded in within a multidimensional, shared-information space to which all the members of the group belong. Except in the most trivial case, members of the collective are affected by changes in the multidimensional space by virtue of their membership. This notion implies that non-trivial collectives exist in causal time. 
 - It seems likely that this collectivism is implied by all of our source frameworks. However, an explicit mention may be particularly necessary in connection to the superorganism concept.
- @Canciani et al. (2019) argue that eusocial insects form hierarchical organizations and that these organizations are emblematic of superorganisms. We disagree. Eusocial insects work, in particular, using shared information (the hierarchy concept ignores synergistic information). This leads to the question: is there a need to explicitly name shared information in the sense of @Daniels et al. (2016); or, is it implied by the other frameworks. The most likely implication is through @Hoel2025 / causality. It seems obvious that all organisms (certainly all organisms with nervous systems) have a shared information space, but this may need to be made explicit specifically to buttress the superorganism claim.
- Consciousness (as a phenomenon; as a concept) is orthogonal. The framework makes structural claims (information, closure, causality), not experiential ones. This sidesteps IIT and the hard problem entirely.
- The lack of constraint closure in superorganisms is a feature for engineering, not a bug: it means components are replaceable, upgradeable, and heterogeneous.
- Connection to capstone: the capstone testbed provides the first empirical measurements of collective properties (amplification, decomposition, criticality) in a multi-agent LLM system, which are the operational signatures of superorganism-hood.
